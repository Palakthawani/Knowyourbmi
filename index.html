LET US KNOW WHAT IS BMI AND HOW TO CALCULATE IT-

BMI refers to Body Mass Index. Body mass index (BMI) is a tool that healthcare providers use to estimate the amount of body fat by using your height and weight measurements. It is used to classify whether a person is underweight, normal weight, overweight or obese.

THE FORMULA TO CALCULATE BMI:
BMI = Weight(kg) / Height(m)2

UNDERWEIGHT (BMI < 18.5)

Goal: Healthy weight gain — build muscle, not just fat.

Breakfast: Peanut butter toast + banana + full cream milk
Mid-morning snack: Dry fruits (almonds, dates, raisins)
Lunch: Rice or roti + dal + sabzi + ghee + curd
Evening snack: Smoothie or cheese sandwich
Dinner: Paneer/chicken + roti + vegetables + sweet potato
Before bed: Warm milk with honey

NORMAL WEIGHT (BMI 18.5–24.9)

Goal: Maintain a balanced lifestyle.

Breakfast: Oats with milk or poha
Snack: Boiled egg or fruit
Lunch: Roti + sabzi + dal + curd
Evening: Buttermilk or roasted chana
Dinner: Light sabzi + roti or soup

OVERWEIGHT (BMI 25–29.9)

Goal: Gentle, steady fat loss.

Breakfast: Vegetable oats or eggs
Snack: Green tea + fruit
Lunch: Roti + dal + sabzi + salad
Snack: Makhana or curd
Dinner: Grilled paneer + veggies or soup

OBESE (BMI 30+)

Goal: Improve blood sugar and fat loss.

Breakfast: Eggs or tofu + spinach
Snack: Carrot sticks or green tea
Lunch: Salad + paneer/chicken
Snack: Black coffee or apple
Dinner: Clear soup + steamed veggies

HIGHLIGHTS

✅ Drink 2–3 liters water daily
✅ Perform yoga and stay active
✅ Avoid sugar and soft drinks
